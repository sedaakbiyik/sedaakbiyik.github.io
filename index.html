I'm Seda Karakose-Akbiyik, a PhD Candidate in the Department of Psychology at Harvard University, working under the guidance of Professor Alfonso Caramazza. I earned my Bachelor of Arts in Psychology at Bachelor of Arts in Psychology at Boğaziçi University, followed by a Master of Science in Cognitive Psychology at Koç University, where I worked with Professors Fuat Balci and Tilbe Göksun.

Every waking moment, our brain takes on the incredible task of making sense of the world around us. It starts with basic perceptual inputs—like the shapes, colors, and movements we see—and transforms them into a rich understanding of our environment. This process allows us to recognize people, identify objects, and make sense of their actions and interactions.

In my research, I study the cognitive and neural mechanisms underlying these abilities using functional neuroimaging and other cognitive science methods, such as behavioral studies, computational modeling, and neuromodulation. I do so by not only examining the typical brain but also exploring how these mechanisms develop in the context of sensory experiences, such as in blindness, and understanding how they break down in cases of brain damage.

I’m in the final stages of my PhD and am exploring post-doctoral opportunities. If you're interested in potential collaborations or discussions, feel free to send me an email at sakbiyik@fas.harvard.edu.
